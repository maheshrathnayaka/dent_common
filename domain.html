    Research Gap

    Considering the technological aspect of dentistry in Sri Lanka, only a significantly low percentage of dentists use intra oral cameras. Sri Lanka has not embraced the new technologies available yet


    Eventhough a low percentage of dentists are known to use intraoral cameras, dentists who use an interactive patient management software system incorporated with the intraoral cameras were not discovered

    Such software systems are most likely to be available in private hospitals but currently, definitely not used in government hospitals and not possessed individually by dentists for their use

    Information technology researches carried out for the field of dentistry are very low and almost none in Sri Lankan context

    When the dental software systems available in the global market were analysed, it was discovered that systems were available for main features needed for dental activities separately. Each system focused only on one main activity related to dentistry. Systems were available separately for treatment procedure explaining, appointment scheduling, online booking, patient management, marketing. However, a comprehensive system addressing all activities was not found in the market

    Research Problem

    The main reasons for the high prevalence of oral diseases were identified to be negligence, poor hygiene, poor education, harmful habits and unavailability of dental health facilities. By addressing these factors, it is believed that oral diseases can be prevented greatly

    Negligence can be addressed by an effective patient management system which sends reminders to patients, thus motivating them to attend regular checkups

    Poor hygiene, poor education, harmful habits could be addressed by a system which provides a clear visual output of the patient's condition. The patient will be able to comprehend the dentist's explanations more clearly with the help of the visual output provided, thus facilitating an effective educational process for the patient

    In Sri Lankan context, a main problem faced is the poor distribution of medical personnel and facilities through the island. The ratio of dental surgeons as of 2012 to the number of patients was 1 : 1667. The major institutions related to dentistry and oral health are located in Colombo, Maharagama, Peradeniya. This causes a great deal of inconvenience to citizens living away from these cities(According to Sri Lanka Annual Health Bulletin 2012)

    There is a clear need for a teleconferencing system which could connect dentists in areas outside of these main cities to these institutions to obtain remote assistance in treating patients
